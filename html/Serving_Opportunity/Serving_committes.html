Committees

Dhamma Delaware is administered by a Trust made up of dedicated old students. The day to day work of running Dhamma Delaware is performed by various committees, some of which are described below. There are various committee tasks for which we are seeking regular volunteers.

On-Site Committee

Coordinates all activities at the center for the smooth running of courses. This committee is made up of various small teams with specific roles and responsibilies. These teams are as follows:

  • Maintenance Team:

    Coordinates the maintenance of the interior and exterior of the centers buildings. Duties include painting, minor electrical and plumbing repairs, and overall maintenance of tools and equipment and machinery.

  • Kitchen Team:

    Orders food for courses, orients staff servers on each course, develops and updates menus and coordinates meals for workdays courses and in-between courses. Other responsibilities include improving the kitchen facilities, maintenance of kitchen equipment, and generating ideas for better organization and efficiency.

  • Household Team:

    Oversees the cleaning and upkeep of all building interiors and furnishings. Responsibilities include coordination of end-of- course student clean-up, linens, laundry, articles for students use, and ordering and stocking of supplies.

  • Grounds and Garden Team:

    Oversees lawn mowing, snow blowing and shoveling as well as landscaping. Maintains existing flower beds. Takes care of indoor plants.

  • Technology/Website Committee

    Maintains all the communications networks at the center including telephone system, computer network and security setup. Maintains the office computers and the center computer network. Maintains several Dhamma Delaware websites.

  • Tent Committee

    Oversees setting up, taking down, storage and maintenance of all Dhamma Delaware tents, tent platforms and tarps

Communications Committee (Inreach/Outreach)

Oversees communiques, brochures, website information going to old or new students. Good writers and graphic artists are needed! Announces course dates to the public through media and posters, and organizes events such as open-houses, public talks, and video presentations.

Dhamma Service Committee

Develops contact with old students interested in service and other center activities, sends announcements for upcoming opportunities to give service, maintains a database of those interested in serving, registers servers and ensures that courses are adequately staffed, and refers interested students to committees.
