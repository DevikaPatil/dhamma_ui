Committees

Dhamma Dharā is administered by a Trust made up of dedicated old students. The day to day work of running Dhamma Dhara is performed by various committees, some of which are described below. There are various committee tasks for which we are seeking regular volunteers.

Maintenance Committee

Coordinates the maintenance of the interior and exterior of the centers buildings. Duties include painting, minor electrical and plumbing repairs, and overall maintenance of tools and equipment and machinery.

Kitchen Committee

Orders food for courses, orients staff on each course, develops and updates menus and coordinates meals for workdays and between courses. Other responsibilities include improving the kitchen facilities, maintenance of kitchen equipment, and generating ideas for better organization and efficiency.

Household Committee

Oversees the cleaning and upkeep of all building interiors and furnishings. Responsibilities include coordination of end-of-course student clean-up, linens, laundry, articles for students use, and ordering and stocking of supplies.

Grounds and Garden Committee

Oversees lawn mowing, snow blowing and shoveling as well as landscaping. Maintains existing flower beds. Takes care of indoor plants.

Technology/Website Committee

Maintains the office computers and the center computer network. Maintains several Dhamma Dharā websites.

Tent Committee

Oversees setting up, taking down, storage and maintenance of all Dhamma Dharā tents, tent platforms and tarps

Outreach Committee

Announces course dates to the public through media and posters, and organizes events such as open-houses, public talks, and video presentations.

Communications Committee

Oversees communiques, brochures, website information going to old or new students. Good writers and graphic artists are needed!

Dhamma Service/Inreach Committee

Develops contact with old students interested in service and other center activities, sends announcements for upcoming opportunities to give service, maintains a database of those interested in serving, registers servers and ensures that courses are adequately staffed, and refers interested students to committees.
