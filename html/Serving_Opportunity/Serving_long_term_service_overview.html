Long-Term Service Overview

Giving Dhamma service is one of the most valuable experiences a student of Vipassana can have. Not only is it rewarding to know that you have helped others to taste the nectar of Dhamma, but it also is a wonderful source of growth and strength for your own meditation practice.

Long-term Dhamma service is for those who wish to develop their paramis by serving for longer periods of time. It gives students the opportunity to bring depth and maturity to their meditation practice by integrating what they have learned during courses and applying it while serving. Students will learn to serve properly with an attitude of humility and renunciation. Long-term service is intended for students who are able to work hard in a surrendered and co-operative way, and who are eager to learn how best to serve in this unique Dhamma environment. This extended period of service provides a great opportunity to deepen one’s practice as well as helping the center to run smoothly.

As a long-term server, you will begin your stay by being involved directly with serving courses, either as a kitchen worker or course manager. As you become more familiar with the center you may be asked to take on more responsibility by getting involved with the committee structure and general operations of the center.

There are two Long Term Service programs at Dhamma Pubbananda (Delaware):

  • Our Sit-Serve program allows students to be in residence for a number of months, participating in courses as a student and as a server, with a greater emphasis on serving. Students may serve two courses, then sit one course and then serve again, depending on the length and type of courses and how well each long-term server is integrating their experience. Assistant Teachers will be available to discuss servers’ development in Dhamma and the integration of their practice. To qualify for this program, a student must first sit and then serve a course at Dhamma Pubbananda (Delaware).
  • Our Off-Site Long Term Service program is for students who can commit to 6 months or longer of service to the center. Here students are allowed to live in our long-term servers’ residences outside the course compound. Long-term servers may serve courses as well as take part in different jobs like course management support, registration, maintenance, grounds & garden, household, and contacting volunteers to serve directly on our courses. Meals are provided at the center and segregated lodging is provided on adjacent properties nearby. Assistant Teachers will be available to discuss server’ development in Dhamma and the integration of their practice.

    • Sincere old students who are practicing this technique exclusively and maintaining the five precepts in their daily life are eligible for Off-site Long-term service at Dhamma Pubbananda (Delaware). An additional requirement is completion of the Sit-Serve residential program at Dhamma Pubbananda (Delaware).

    Many questions that you may have pertaining to Long-term Service can be answered by reading the Long Term Dhamma Service FAQ. Any old student may apply. Please understand that acceptance is contingent upon approval of an assistant teacher and center management. The center reserves the option to ask a student to leave at any time if their long term service is not meeting the student’s or center’s needs. If interested, please write to manager@delaware.dhamma.org

    Positions

    The following positions are available for serious old students who have sat and served a number of courses, have a basic understanding of how the center operates, and are ready to take on more responsibility at the center.

    Kitchen Coordinator

    The Kitchen Coordinator is a member of the Onsite Committee – Kitchen team and works with the other members of the committee to ensure that the kitchen functions properly.

    Duties include the day to day operation of the kitchen, integrating and orienting kitchen servers, over-seeing kitchen tasks between courses, keeping track of the food inventory, placing food orders, receiving food deliveries, and keeping menus up to date. The coordinator will attend a food safety certification course at the center’s expense.

    Household Coordinator

    The Household Coordinator is a member of the Onsite Committee - Household team and works with the other members of the committee to ensure that the center is a clean and welcoming environment for students.

    Duties include coordinating the student clean up on Day 11, walking through the center on Day 11 and Day 0 in order to assess which areas have been cleaned and which areas still need cleaning, over-seeing household tasks between courses, and orienting servers who are new to working with the Household team.

    Maintenance Coordinator

    The Maintenance Coordinator is a member of the Onsite Committee, and works with other members of the committee. The primary responsibility of this position is maintaining the center’s buildings and physical operations.

    This entails troubleshooting problems with the physical plant when they arise, implementing repairs when possible & contacting the appropriate sources when not, keeping an up-to-date list of the various maintenance jobs around the center, and coordinating servers interested in doing maintenance related work at the center. Any carpentry, painting, plumbing, or electrical skills would be helpful in this position.
