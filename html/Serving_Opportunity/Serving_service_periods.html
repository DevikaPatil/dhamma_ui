The Dhamma Service Period has a relaxed schedule that includes three daily group sittings, work on seasonal projects, and various evening programs designed to both inform and inspire old students. This DSP program is for all old students who have sat at least one course with S. N. Goenka or one of his assistant teachers and who have not practiced another meditation technique since their last course. Any such old student is welcome to serve all, or part, of the program.

The Center will soon enter the Phase III of Center development. This phase will include the complete renovation of building 3. The building is expected to provide a better and more conducive environment for students to meditate in and deepen their practice. Students will have their own private rooms with attached bathrooms to help reduce distractions. Besides this there will be individual cells available for students to meditate in. Goenkaji said that an ideal meditation center should have individual rooms and cells available for each student to meditate in. In phase III of center development, the cell structure will include 22 cells in building 3. Another 22 cells will be added during phase IV in building 4. The cell structures will be crowned with a small golden pagoda (zedi in Burmese language). During the DSP and phase III construction both skilled and unskilled servers are needed to help with the cleanup, framing, assembly and erection of the Pagoda.

There is a great deal of work to be done this year as we will be deep cleaning the interiors, doing some extensive gardening/landscaping and many more projects which cannot be done while courses are in progress; it’s an exciting time to be at the center! In addition to the three daily group sittings there will also be evening programs and informational presentations.

Please apply if you have the time and volition to serve. We hope to see you soon.

Please feel free to e-mail us at dhamma-service@delaware.dhamma.org or call the center if you have any questions.
